1. Introduction & Scope

This Privacy Policy describes how the Law Offices of Arun Kumar ("the Firm", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, discloses, retains, and protects personal data in connection with this website (the "Website"), and any communications made to us through email, WhatsApp, telephone, or other channels referenced on the Website.

This Policy is framed with reference to the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDP Act") and the Digital Personal Data Protection Rules made thereunder, as applicable in India. By accessing or using the Website, or by voluntarily submitting personal data to us through any of the contact channels listed on the Website, you ("you", "user", or "Data Principal") acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy.

This Website is maintained for informational purposes only, consistent with the Rules of the Bar Council of India framed under the Advocates Act, 1961. Nothing in this Policy, and no interaction with this Website, creates an advocate-client relationship.

3. Personal Data We Collect

We collect personal data only where it is voluntarily provided by you, or where it is generated in the ordinary course of your interaction with the Website. This may include:

  • Contact information you choose to share, such as your name, email address, telephone/WhatsApp number, and postal address, when you write to us, call us, or message us.
  • Correspondence data — the content of any email, WhatsApp message, or other communication you send to the Firm, including any documents or attachments you choose to share.
  • Technical and usage data, such as your device type, browser type, approximate location derived from IP address, and pages viewed, which may be collected automatically by standard web-server logs or, if enabled in future, by analytics tools.

We do not knowingly collect any sensitive category of personal data (such as financial information, health data, or biometric data) through the Website itself. Any such information shared voluntarily by you in the course of seeking legal assistance is handled under the heightened confidentiality obligations applicable to the advocate-client relationship, once and if such a relationship is separately and expressly established.

4. How and Why We Process Your Personal Data

We process personal data only for specified, lawful purposes for which you have given consent, or which are otherwise permitted under the DPDP Act. These purposes include:

  • Responding to enquiries sent to us via email, WhatsApp, telephone, or any contact form on the Website;
  • Maintaining records of correspondence for professional, administrative, and legal record-keeping purposes;
  • Complying with applicable law, professional obligations under the Advocates Act, 1961 and the Bar Council of India Rules, and any lawful direction of a court, tribunal, or regulatory authority;
  • Operating, securing, and improving the functioning of the Website.

We do not use personal data collected through the Website for advertising, solicitation of legal work, or any purpose inconsistent with the Rules of the Bar Council of India.

5. Consent

Where processing of your personal data is based on consent, such consent is free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous, given through a clear affirmative action — for example, by voluntarily sending us an email, initiating a WhatsApp conversation, or calling our office. You may withdraw your consent at any time, with the same ease with which it was given, by writing to us at the email address in Section 2. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before such withdrawal, and may limit our ability to respond to a pending enquiry.

6. Disclosure of Personal Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data. Personal data may be disclosed only in the following limited circumstances:

  • To professional associates, co-counsel, or support staff engaged by the Firm, strictly on a need-to-know basis and subject to confidentiality obligations, where relevant to a matter you have instructed us on;
  • To courts, tribunals, statutory authorities, or regulators, where disclosure is required by law or by an order of a competent authority;
  • To service providers who process data on our behalf (for example, email or website-hosting providers), who are contractually or otherwise bound to maintain confidentiality and use the data solely for the purpose for which it was shared.

7. Cross-Border Transfer of Personal Data

The Website may be hosted using infrastructure or service providers located outside India (for example, cloud-hosting or email-service providers). Where personal data is transferred outside India in this manner, such transfer is undertaken in accordance with the DPDP Act, and to jurisdictions that are not restricted by the Central Government under the Act as may be notified from time to time.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purpose for which it was collected, or as required to comply with our legal, regulatory, professional, or record-keeping obligations, including those arising under the Advocates Act, 1961 and applicable limitation statutes. Where personal data is no longer necessary for these purposes and there is no other legal basis to retain it, we will take reasonable steps to erase it or render it non-identifiable.

9. Security Safeguards

We implement reasonable technical and organisational security safeguards to protect personal data in our possession or control against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or loss, as contemplated under the DPDP Act. These include restricting access to correspondence on a need-to-know basis and using reputable, secure communication and hosting service providers. No method of electronic transmission or storage is entirely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Subject to the provisions of the DPDP Act and the Rules made thereunder, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data processed by us:

  • Right to access information about the personal data we hold about you and the processing activities undertaken;
  • Right to correction and updating of inaccurate or incomplete personal data;
  • Right to erasure of personal data that is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, subject to any legal obligation requiring its retention;
  • Right to grievance redressal in relation to any act or omission of the Firm regarding the performance of its obligations under the DPDP Act;
  • Right to nominate another individual to exercise these rights on your behalf in the event of your death or incapacity.

To exercise any of these rights, please write to us at the email address in Section 2, providing sufficient detail to allow us to verify your identity and locate the relevant personal data. We will respond within a reasonable time and in accordance with the timelines, if any, prescribed under the DPDP Rules.

11. Children's Personal Data

This Website is not directed at, and is not intended for use by, children (individuals below the age of 18 years). We do not knowingly collect personal data of children through the Website. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data of a child without verifiable parental or guardian consent, we will take steps to delete such data at the earliest opportunity.

12. Cookies & Website Analytics

The Website, in its current form, does not use tracking cookies or third-party advertising technologies. Standard web-server logs may record technical information such as IP address, browser type, and access times for security and operational purposes. Should the Firm introduce analytics or cookie-based tools in the future, this Policy will be updated accordingly and, where required, appropriate consent mechanisms will be implemented.
